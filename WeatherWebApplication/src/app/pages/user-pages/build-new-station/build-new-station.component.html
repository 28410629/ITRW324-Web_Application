Guide Hardware-Requirements

These are the items you need to build the station with:

Veraboard NodeMCU ESP8266 E-12 BME280 Sensor TEMT6000 Light Sensor 18650 TP4056 Lithium Battery Charger Solar Panel (0.66W, 5.5V, 120mAh) NCR18650A Battery (3.6V, 3100mAh) 2x15 piece female headers ***** 1x4 piece female header ***** 1x3 piece female header ***** 4x1 female headers ***** 4 jumper cables to be used for wiring ***** 1 jumper cables with male headers 4 copper wires 7 male header pins Soldering Iron & Soldering Computer for software flashing Working Internet Skill-Requirements
  • Skill level needed: Beginner
  • Technological hardware know-how
  • Should be able to read
 Time-Requirements

 Software-Requirements Arduino IDE Hardware Installation Please se attached figure for any reference to NodeMCU orientation:



    Step 1: Soldering Sensors

     Solder 4 male header pins to BME Sensor Solder 3 male header pins to TEMT Light Sensor

    Step 2: Soldering NodMCU3
    After soldering is done:

    Insert 2x15 piece female headers to veraboard, ensure enough space for NodeMCu to and rest of the components to fit, as well as headers being perpendicular to veraboard Solder female headers to veraboard, ensure pins do not connect next to each other when soldering To ensure no connectivety, break flow between two sides of pin through scratching copper plating of board, or other means and preferably from side to side ******* Insert NodeMCU, making sure it fits comfortably *****

    Step 3: Solder BME280-Sensor
    After soldering male header pins on BME280 Sensor:

    Insert 1x4 piece female header pins to veraboard, preferably on right on NodeMCU, allowing SDA and SCL pin on BME Sensor to connect to D1 and D0 pin on NodeMcu respectively and ensuring pins are perpendicular to veraboard. Solder 1x4 piece female header pin to veraboard, ensuring pins dont connect next to each other Insert BME Sensor, ensuring it fits comfortably Connect SDA pin on BME Sensor to D2 pin on the right side of the NodeMCU [Not needed]

    Step 4: Solder TEMT6000 Light Sensor
    After soldering male header pins on TEMT6000 Light Sensor:

    Insert 1x3 piece female header pins to veraboard, preferably on left on NodeMCU, allowing S pin on TEMT Sensor to connect to A0 pin on NodeMcu respectively and ensuring pins are perpendicular to veraboard. Solder 1x3 piece female header pin to veraboard, ensuring pins dont connect next to each other Insert TEMT Sensor, ensuring it fits comfortably

    Step 5: Enabling Deepsleep
    Afterwards, to enable deepsleep for minimizing battery consumption:

    Solder a male header jumper cable pin to a lane connecting to the D0 pin on the left side of the NodeMCU and the other end to a lane connecting to a AST pin on the right side, test connectivity
